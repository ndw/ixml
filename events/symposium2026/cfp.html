The First International Symposium on Invisible XML

26/27 February 2026, online

Call for Presentations

We invite proposals from new users and experts alike. Share your experiences, techniques, ideas, implementations, use cases, grammars, and anything else ixml-related.

Presentations will be by Zoom. Timeslots are flexible, from ten to 30 minutes in length, so presenters can judge how long their talk requires.

Proposals should include a title, requested timeslot duration, and summary (100–200 words).

There is no requirement for a written paper. Presenters will be asked to submit a copy of their slides after the event. With presenter permission, talks will be recorded and the videos published.

You can submit your proposals to submit@invisiblexml.org, on or before November 30, 2025.

We have recently noted that the use of large language models (LLMs) and similar "generative AI" tools has become very common in talks related to markup technologies. This symposium will apply two requirements:

  1. authors of any accepted proposal which makes use of an LLM or other "generative AI" tool must sign a statement that the tool has been trained on data acquired legally and with the informed consent of its original creators;
  2. talks must not be written, in whole or in part, by an LLM.

Please get in touch with the committee on submit@invisiblexml.org if you have any queries about this policy and how it might apply to your submission.

Accepted presenters can contact the committee if they would like to discuss or ask for advice about any aspect of their presentation. Please contact the committee before 19 February to ensure that there is time for discussion before the symposium.
